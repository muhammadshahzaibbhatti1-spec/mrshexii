The Weight of Being Understood

سمجھے جانے کا بوجھ

There are people who enter your life and rearrange the furniture of your soul without asking permission. You wake up one day and nothing is where you left it — and somehow, impossibly, it feels more like home than before.

I have spent most of my life trying to explain myself to people who were never really listening. Not out of cruelty — they just had their own noise. Their own weather. And I was just another sound in it.

I became very good at abbreviating myself. At giving people the edited version. The one that fits comfortably into a conversation without requiring too much of them. I packaged myself in ways that made me easier to hold.

There is something both relieving and devastating about being truly seen. Relieving because you no longer have to perform. Devastating because you realise how long you have been performing.
جو مجھے سمجھے وہ مجھ سے ڈرتا بھی ہے
کیونکہ وہ جانتا ہے میں کیا ہوں

And then one day, someone arrived who heard what I hadn't even said yet. Who looked at the version of me I was presenting and said — quietly, without drama — that they could see the other one too. The unabbreviated one.

That kind of recognition is terrifying. It rearranges you.

✦

The Problem with Being Found

The problem with being found is that you can no longer pretend to be lost. You cannot retreat into the comfortable smallness of being misunderstood. Someone has mapped you. Someone has memorised your geography.

I think that is why some of us keep choosing people who cannot quite reach us. There is safety in the gap. In the space between who you are and who they think you are.

But I am tired of living in that gap. I am tired of being a rumour of myself.

Ghalib once wrote that thousands of desires live in him, each one worth dying for. I wonder if being truly understood was one of them — and whether he feared it as much as he wanted it.

Maybe this is what poetry is for. Not to be understood by a single person in a single moment — but to send your most honest self forward into time, and trust that somewhere, someone will receive it.

This blog is that trust.
